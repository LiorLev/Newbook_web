    • Our People

    Newbook Digital Texts in the Humanities was founded in 2012, by Walter Andrews, at the University of Washington. It has come to include a group of scholars who envision easy, open access to less well known literary and historical texts, and like works in the humanities and social sciences that might not otherwise be published by conventional academic presses.

    Directors & Board

    Walter G. Andrews

    walter@uw.edu

    Founder of the Ottoman Text Archive Project, and co-founder of Newbook Digital Texts in the Humanities. Walter is a Research Professor of Near Eastern Languages and Literature; he has translated and published several works on Ottoman and modern Turkish poetry and culture.

    Mary E. Childs, PhD

    mchilds@uw.edu

    co-founder of Newbook Digital Texts in the Humanities. Mary is a specialist in late- and post-Soviet Literature, both Russian and Georgian, and gender studies. She is a translator of Georgian literature, and a lecturer at the University of Washington, both in the Slavic Department and in Comparative History of Ideas; and an adjunct Professor of Russian Culture at Seattle Pacific University.

    Sarah Ketchley, PhD

    ketchley@uw.edu

    co-founder of Newbook Digital Texts in the Humanities. Sarah is an Egyptologist, specializing in art history in the first millennium. She has taught courses Egyptian history and archeology at the University of Birmingham, UK, and at the University of Washington. She is currently working on Emma B. Andrews Diaries, inspired by a passion for intrepid women travelers in the 19th century.

    Consultant

    Stacy Waters, PhD

    stacy@uw.edu

    provides technical consulting to Walter Andrews on the Ottoman Text Archive Project and Svoboda Diary projects. He has taught courses in Digital Arts for the Humanities, and provided consulting for projects at CARTAH (Center for Advanced Research Technology in the Arts and Humanities), at the University of Washington. He is a specialist in medieval English texts.

    Technical Staff

    Rachel Brown

    elle.brown@gmail.com

    an MA Candidate in the NELC department at the University of Washington. She works as the project manager for the Svoboda Diaries project, and overseas its day-to-day running. Rachel's research areas include Iraq, Iran, Syria and Turkey and her language focuses are in Turkish, Persian and French.

    Ryan Bielby

    rbielby@u.washington.edu

    an MS Candidate in Computational Linguistics at the University of Washington, with an interest in Georgian language, machine translation, and hiking.

    Joshua Crowgy

    jcrowgey@uw.edu

    a PhD student in Computational Linguistics at the University of Washington. He has been assisting the Svoboda Diary Project, Ottoman Text Archive Project, and the Alexander Diaries for four years. His special interests include formal syntax, Salish languages (e.g. Lushootseed), linguistic typology, and gardening.
